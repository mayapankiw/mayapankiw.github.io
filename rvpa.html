RV–PA Coupling Analysis Pipeline

Problem: Right heart failure is a major complication after heart transplantation or LVAD implantation in patients experiencing heart failure

Evaluation: Current evaluation relies on invasive hemodynamic assessment, but lacks quantitative tools to assess RV contractile reserve

Right ventricular-pulmonary artery coupling provides a physiological measure of cardiac function by relating contractility to afterload

This work presents an algorithm derived from clinically available signals, designed to support transplant decision-making in advanced heart failure patients

Algorithm Highlights:

  • Processed ECG, RV, PA, and RA waveforms for synchronized multi-signal analysis
  • Developed algorithms for physiologic feature detection and cardiac phase segmentation
  • Derived pressure-based metrics to estimate RV contractility and RV-PA coupling
  • Designed pipeline for clinical interpretability and reproducibility
