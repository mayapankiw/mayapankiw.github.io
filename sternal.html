Sternal Closure Testing Apparatus

Background: Median sternotomy is a surgical approach that requires stable closure of the sternum to enable proper postoperative healing

Problem: Inadequate sternal fixation can lead to sternal separation under physiological loading, increasing the risk of serious complications

Standardized, physiologically relevant testing is required to evaluate and compare emerging sternal closure devices under realistic loading conditions

This work presents the development of a sternal closure testing apparatus designed to replicate physiological respiratory loading and enable quantitative evaluation of sternal fixation techniques

Highlights of Testing System:

  • Designed mechanical system to apply controlled cyclic loading to sternal specimens
  • Machined custom components using manual equipment including mill, lathe, and drill press
  • Developed CAD models and engineering drawings in accordance with GD&T standards
  • Integrated system with Instron testing equipment for combined experimental workflows
  • Supported development of repeatable testing protocols for biomechanical evaluation

System simulates breathing-like cyclic loading and applies physiologic forces to replicate thoracic mechanics during post-surgical healing. The original capstone project aimed to create a standardized and reusable platform for evaluating novel sternal closure devices under clinically relevant loading conditions.
