About Me

I am a graduate student at the University of Toronto working in the Cardiac Catheterization Research Laboratory at Mount Sinai Hospital. My work focuses on cardiac engineering, hemodynamic modelling, invasive waveform analysis, and signal processing for clinically relevant applications.

My current research centers on the development and validation of an algorithm to estimate right ventricular–pulmonary arterial coupling from invasive pressure data, with the goal of improving physiologic assessment in advanced heart failure.

Project

RV:PA Coupling Algorithm

Developed a signal processing and hemodynamic analysis pipeline in Python to estimate right ventricular–pulmonary arterial coupling using invasive pressure waveforms collected during right heart catheterization.

This project includes ECG synchronization, pressure waveform preprocessing, feature detection, physiologic windowing, ensemble averaging, and derivation of pressure-based indices used to characterize RV performance and RV–PA interaction.

  • Processed ECG, RV, PA, and RA waveform data for physiologic feature extraction
  • Developed algorithms for identifying waveform landmarks and timing relationships
  • Supported clinical interpretation through quantitative hemodynamic modelling

Project

Sternal Closure Testing Apparatus

Designed and developed a mechanical testing apparatus for evaluating sternal closure performance as part of my undergraduate capstone project.

This work involved CAD design, mechanical system development, iterative prototyping, and design considerations for repeatable experimental testing.

  • Created detailed CAD models of the testing system
  • Contributed to mechanical design and apparatus development
  • Supported evaluation of closure performance under controlled testing conditions
